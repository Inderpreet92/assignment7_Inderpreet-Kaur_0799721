Marine Commandos

In this image there are two marine commandos. There are three renowned Marine Commando units from different countries

  • United States Marine Corps Force Reconnaissance (Force Recon):
    • United States Marine Corps Force Reconnaissance (Force Recon)
    Force Reconnaissance, commonly known as Force Recon, is a specialized reconnaissance unit within the United States Marine Corps. Force Recon Marines are highly trained in amphibious reconnaissance, direct action, surveillance, and intelligence gathering. They operate ahead of conventional forces, gathering critical information about enemy movements, terrain, and other factors essential for operational planning. Force Recon units have been involved in numerous combat operations and have a reputation for their exceptional skills and professionalism.
  • British Royal Marines Special Boat Service (SBS):
    • British Royal Marines Special Boat Service (SBS)
    The Special Boat Service (SBS) is a special forces unit of the British Royal Marines. While the Royal Marines specialize in amphibious operations, the SBS focuses specifically on maritime counter-terrorism, direct action, and reconnaissance missions. SBS operators undergo rigorous training in combat swimming, small boat operations, and specialized insertion techniques. They have been involved in various high-profile operations, including counter-terrorism missions and reconnaissance in support of British military objectives worldwide.
  • Indian Marine Commandos (MARCOS):
    • Indian Marine Commandos (MARCOS)
    The Marine Commandos (MARCOS) are the special operations force of the Indian Navy. Formed in 1987, MARCOS are trained to conduct maritime special operations, including reconnaissance, direct action, counter-terrorism, and hostage rescue missions. MARCOS operators are skilled in underwater combat, combat diving, and small team tactics. They have played a crucial role in several counter-terrorism operations and have a reputation for their expertise in maritime warfare and special operations in coastal and littoral regions.
